Machine Learning

Machine learning (ML) is an umbrella term for solving problems for which development of algorithms by human programmers would be cost-prohibitive, and instead the problems are solved by helping machines 'discover' their 'own' algorithms,[1] without needing to be explicitly told what to do by any human-developed algorithms.[2] When there was a vast amount of potential answers, the correct ones needed to be labeled as valid by human labelers initially and human supervision was needed. As computational power has increased and deep learning become more powerful, machines have been able to 'discover' their own models, using supervised learning, unsupervised learning and reinforcement learning.[3][4][5] Recently, generative artificial neural networks have been able to surpass results of many previous approaches.[6][7] The applications include computer vision, speech recognition, email filtering, agriculture and medicine, where it is too costly to develop algorithms to perform the needed tasks.
